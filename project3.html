01 — Problems

Safety Shouldn't Feel Like Work

Women in India face genuine, daily safety concerns — yet most safety tools are either too cumbersome to use in a moment of panic, or too limited to provide real protection. Existing solutions scatter critical features across multiple apps, require too many steps under stress, and offer no intelligent, proactive support.

01

Too Many Steps in a Crisis

Existing safety apps require unlocking a phone, finding the app, and navigating menus — in situations where every second counts. The interaction cost is dangerously high.

02

No Intelligent Threat Detection

Most apps are purely reactive — they wait for the user to act. There is no proactive system that monitors context (voice, location, time) and initiates help before the user can.

03

Fragmented Emergency Contacts

Calling a trusted contact, sending location, and alerting police are three separate actions across three different interfaces. In a real emergency, no one has that cognitive bandwidth.

04

No Real-time Location Sharing

Sharing your location means opening a map app, copying a link, and sending it. Live, automatic location sharing triggered by an SOS — with no manual steps — didn't exist.

05

Fake Helpline Numbers

Many users were unsure which helpline numbers to call in different situations. Scattered, hard-to-recall numbers reduced the likelihood of reaching help quickly.

06

Fear of Drawing Attention

Visibly using a safety app in a dangerous situation can escalate risk. Users needed discreet features — fake calls, silent SOS triggers — to act without tipping off a threat.

02 — Ideation & Research

Listening to Women's Real Experiences

Before wireframing, I conducted qualitative interviews with 15 women aged 18–32 across college campuses and urban environments in India. The goal wasn't to find feature requests — it was to understand the emotional texture of feeling unsafe, and what kind of interface could feel like a trusted companion rather than a tool.

When I'm scared, I can't think clearly. I need one button that does everything. — Student, 20, Delhi
I've called the wrong number in panic. I just need the right one to be right there. — Professional, 27, Noida
I wish I could just say "help" and my family would know where I am instantly. — Student, 19, Greater Noida
I need to look calm, but still trigger something. Taking out a phone looks obvious. — Student, 22, Gurgaon

Five clear design principles emerged from this research and guided every subsequent decision:

🆘 One action must trigger everything
Discreet interaction is a safety feature
AI must watch when the user can't act
Location must be automatic, not manual
💗 The interface must feel calm, not alarming
03 — Wireframes

Version 1 — Basic Safety Grid

The first concept was a simple grid of safety actions: Share Location, Fake Call, Call Police, and Guardian toggle. Clean and fast to reach — but fundamentally missing intelligence and context. It was reactive, not proactive.

The First Draft

Version 1 presented four primary action tiles on the home screen with a search bar and basic navigation. An SOS button floated at the bottom. Emergency helplines were listed below the grid.

Usability tests revealed the design had serious gaps when stress-tested against real-use scenarios.

  • 1No Safety Status: Users had no way to communicate their current state. No "I'm safe" signal meant contacts had no baseline to measure against.
  • 2Guardian Toggle was Confusing: The Guardian feature appeared as an on/off card with no explanation — users didn't understand what it activated or why it mattered.
  • 3No AI Integration: The app was purely manual. There was no voice monitoring, no automatic threat detection, no late-night risk flagging.
Version 1

Version 2 — AI-Powered Guardian

Version 2 introduced live status indicators, AI Guardian settings with granular controls, a trusted contacts system with real contact management, and safety tips embedded contextually. The app evolved from a reactive tool to a proactive safety companion.

Three Screens, One Coherent Safety System

The redesign introduced live status badges, AI Guardian toggles with clear labels, a full contacts management flow, and granular control over safety features like Voice Monitoring and Shake-to-SOS.

Each screen reinforces the same visual language — deep magenta gradient, rounded cards, clear toggles — so the app feels instantly familiar regardless of which screen a user is on under stress.

Design decision: The AI Guardian settings were given their own dedicated section in the Profile screen rather than buried in Settings — making it easy to activate and review without hunting through menus.
04 — Solution

A Companion That's Always Ready

Rakshak combines proactive AI monitoring with one-tap emergency response, creating a safety net that works even when the user cannot consciously act. Every feature was designed to minimise interaction load and maximise protection.

Feature 01

One-Tap SOS

A single tap on the persistent SOS button simultaneously calls the police, sends SMS alerts to all trusted contacts, and shares a live location link. Three emergency actions collapse into one gesture — removing the cognitive load from the most critical moment.

Why it matters: In user interviews, 9/10 women said they couldn't reliably execute a 3-step process under genuine fear. One tap changes that entirely.
Feature 02

AI Guardian System

Voice monitoring detects distress keywords like "Help" or "Bachao" and auto-triggers SOS. Location Risk Check flags high-risk zones automatically. Late-night mode (10PM–4AM) elevates alert sensitivity. The AI watches so the user doesn't have to.

Design decision: Each AI feature is individually toggleable — giving users control and trust while still defaulting to maximum protection on first launch.
Feature 03

Discreet Triggers

Fake Call simulates an incoming call, giving users a socially acceptable excuse to leave a threatening situation. Shake-to-SOS allows the app to be triggered from inside a pocket without visual interaction — protecting users who cannot visibly use their phone.

Feature 04

Trusted Contacts & Helplines

A dedicated contacts screen manages up to 5 trusted people with relationship labels, one-tap call/message, and automatic inclusion in SOS broadcasts. Curated emergency helplines (1091 Women's Helpline, 100 Police) are always one scroll away from the home screen.
