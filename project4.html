02 — Design Process

From Concept to Code

Every decision in this project was intentional — from the ocean backdrop that evokes the depth and clarity of high-fidelity sound, to the exploded view that communicates engineering precision. Here's how it came together.

Step 01 · Brand Identity

Naming & Visual Direction

The name ECHO was chosen to evoke sound reflection, depth, and clarity — all qualities of premium audio. The visual language followed: deep ocean imagery, dark navy tones, white product against moody water. The brand needed to feel premium without being cold.

Direction: High-contrast white product on dark ocean background — borrowed from Apple's product photography language but grounded in nature rather than studio white.
Step 02 · 3D Modelling

Sculpting in Spline

The EchoPods model was built in Spline — starting with the body form, then adding the silicone eartip, driver assembly, and stem. The exploded view animation was created to reveal the internal components — magnet, driver plate, circuit board — giving users an X-ray sense of quality without technical jargon.

Key decision: The gold-toned internal components against the matte white exterior create a luxury contrast that communicates premium engineering.
Step 03 · Figma Web Design

Building the E-commerce Experience

The website was designed in Figma with a focus on product storytelling. The hero section embeds the live Spline model, making the product interactive from the first moment. Below, a structured product grid, feature section, and login screen complete the full e-commerce flow.

Design system: Consistent use of dark cards, white typography, and accent blue kept the interface cohesive across all 8+ screens.
Step 04 · Anima Integration

Figma → Live Website

Anima's Figma plugin was used to export the entire design as a live, responsive web page. The Spline iframe was preserved within the exported code, meaning the 3D model renders interactively on the published site — not as a static image.

Why it matters: This bridges the gap between design prototype and real web product — the site at animaapp.io is not a mockup. It is live and accessible.
03 — 3D Model · Spline

EchoPods — Built in Spline

The EchoPods 3D model was designed to serve two purposes: a hero visual that draws users in, and an exploded diagram that communicates product quality. Every component was modelled with realistic proportions and material properties.

The 3D Spline model may not always load correctly depending on device/browser. A static reference image is provided for consistency.

Component Breakdown

Every part of the model was designed with real-world earphone engineering in mind:

①

Silicone Eartip

Soft, ergonomic seal for passive noise isolation and comfort fit. Modelled with subtle translucency.

②

Acoustic Mesh

Protects the driver from debris while maintaining sound passage. Gold-toned for premium visual cue.

③

Driver Circuit Board

Crossover and signal routing components. Visible circuit traces in the exploded view communicate engineering depth.

④

Dynamic Driver

The core transducer. Modelled as a layered disc with visible voice coil and diaphragm rings.

⑤

Housing Shell

Matte white ABS body. Clean, minimal form with subtle surface curvature for a premium feel.

⑥

Stem & Microphone

Angled stem design for stability. Microphone pinhole detail on the underside for call clarity.

Design Decision A

Ocean as the Brand Canvas

The hero section uses a dark ocean photograph as the backdrop — evoking the depth, clarity, and immersive quality of high-fidelity audio. Water and sound share the same physics: waves, frequency, resonance. The visual metaphor does conceptual work without needing text to explain it.

Design Decision B

White Product on Dark World

The white EchoPods against the near-black ocean background creates an instant visual hierarchy and luxury contrast. It also solves a practical design problem: the Spline iframe's transparent background integrates seamlessly with the dark site canvas — no white box, no awkward cutout.
